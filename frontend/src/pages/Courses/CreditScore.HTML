Understanding Credit: Your Complete Guide

What is a Credit Card?

A credit card is a way to borrow money from a bank. When you use it to buy something, you are using the bank's money. You must pay this money back to the bank later.

Important Facts:
  • The bank tells you how much you can spend
  • You must pay the money back each month
  • If you don't pay all the money back, you pay extra costs
  • The bank sends you a bill each month showing what you bought

What is a Credit Score?

A credit score is a number that shows banks if you are good at paying back money. Think of it like a grade for how you handle money.

Key Points:
  • Scores go from 300 to 850
  • Higher numbers are better
  • Banks look at this number before lending you money
  • Your score changes based on how you use money

What is Payment History?

  • Shows if you pay bills on time
  • Most important part of your credit score
  • Late payments hurt your score a lot
  • Always pay at least the minimum on time

What is Credit Utilization?

  • How much of your credit you use
  • Keep it under 30% for good credit
  • Example: Use less than $300 of a $1,000 limit
  • Lower utilization = better credit score

Credit Card Interest

  • Extra money you pay when you don't pay in full
  • Makes your purchases more expensive
  • Avoid it by paying your full bill monthly
  • Usually very high on credit cards
